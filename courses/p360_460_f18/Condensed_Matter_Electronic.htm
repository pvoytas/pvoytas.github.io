Condensed Matter Electronic

Mark Stahl 11/05/08
 
Condensed matter physics deals with the microscopic properties and behaviors of matter. The subgroup "condensed matter electronics" looks specifically at the properties and behaviors of electrons. 

McGill University recently discovered yet another state of matter. The state is called "quasi-three-dimensional electron crystal." Using lot temperatures (100 time colder than space) and the most powerful "continuous magnetic field on Earth" they form a thin sheet of electrons. Like billiard balls as they explain it. The ramifications on the new state of matter are its effects on the world of transistors. This new state shares some properties with the state currently used in the manufacture of transistors and may allow future designs to become faster, smaller, better.


Superconducting is a term thrown around a lot in the physics world. An ideal supper conductor would have 0 resistance. Current experiments on superconductors are being conducted at extremely cold temperatures, around 50 K (-370 F). For superconductors to be practical in everyday life work is continuously being done in making them smaller and at higher temperatures. The highest temperature superconductor I could find was made by Oak Ridge National Laboratory and operates at 135K (-216.67 F).
