"Physics Minutes" A new thing for junior senior seminar!

Have you ever wondered how your undergraduate physics classes relate to active research fields and subfields of physics? Have you ever been curious about the difference between chemical physics and physical chemistry? This semester in junior senior seminar we're all going to find out together!

In addition to the usual presentations made in seminar, each of you will give one short spot (absolute maximum of five minutes; can be just words with no PowerPoint) on one subfield of physics (or engineering if you are so inclined). Each spot will consist of

1) a brief description of the field or subfield in everyday language (to the extent possible) based on at least three references you've found

2) at least two examples of current problems in this field that are being researched

You may choose from the list below or e-mail me some other field you really want to do. You must let me know at least 1 week ahead of time what your choice is. Topics will be claimed on a first come first served basis, but trading is allowed if you inform me! I'll post a schedule as topics are claimed.

You must also e-mail me the references you found, and a text summary (such as you will be presenting for 1) and 2) above ) and I will archive them on the seminar webpage for future reference (for example, for when you are applying for jobs, or grad school...).
