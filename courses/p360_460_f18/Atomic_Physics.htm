Atomic physics

Today my topic is atomic physics. This is a field of physics studies atoms as an isolated system of electrons and an atomic nucleus. It is concerned with the arrangement of electrons around the nucleus and the processes how to change these arrangements.

The history of atomic physics starts with the discovery of spectral lines and the attempts to describe the phenomenon by Joseph von Fraunhofer. Later the studies of these spectral lines led to Bohr atom model and the birth of quantum mechanics. After the Second World War, atomic physics has dramatically development. The studies of bigger and more sophisticated models of atomic structure and associated collision process were realized by the progress in computing technology. Also technological advances in accelerator, detector, magnetic field generation and laser have greatly assisted the experimental work.

Now, atomic physics is a very broad area. It usually integrated with quantum mechanics, group theory, electricity and magnetism, nuclear physics, surface physics, physical chemistry, fluid dynamics, and plasma physics etc.

Currently there are many experiments/projects deal with atomic physics, and a one I am very interested in a group at the University of Colorado. Since ultra cold atoms and molecules comprise novel forms matter at temperature below a few millionths of a degree above absolute zero. And the group at JILA creates these novel substances and investigates their properties, behavior and interactions. They are trying to answer how do unltracold molecules collide? How precisely can we measure interactions between atoms or molecules in a BEC? What is the connection between BEC and superconductivity? And do electrons have an electric dipole moment? Etc.
