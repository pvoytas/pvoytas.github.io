Peer Review/Scholarly Journals

Library info

In most of the physical sciences and engineering, techinical journals are published by corporations or sometimes professional societies or other organizations.

 

Target audience is generally researchers in the field so usually narrow focus and highly specialized. Exception: Review Articles (RMP, RPP , etc.).

 

Articles submitted for publication are handled by editors who:

    • judge the overall appropriateness of the article for the journal.
    • choose "referees" to review the article. Referees (peer reviewers) are other experts in the field or closely related one which can look for
      • is the work origial?
      • is it presented well/appropriatedly?
      • are the results clear enough and detailed enough that it is clear what process/procedures/evidence the authors are relying on?
      • etc. depending on journal type and guidelines

 

Articles may be refused, accepted, or returned for revision (you thought we made you do rewrites because we're mean--nope, it's real life).

 

Authors get no money for the articles, it is more likely that they have to actually pay to have them published...

 

Articles are sometimes posted as "preprints" on authors' web sites or at places like "arxiv.org" for

a) quick dissemination

b) easy access

c) dissemination of controversial material

d) material not deemed "exciting" enough by journals

e) crazy stuff.

 

Peer review of such material can be good (articles simultaneously going to a journal may be on such sites) to non-existent. So these must be approached with (extreme) caution.
