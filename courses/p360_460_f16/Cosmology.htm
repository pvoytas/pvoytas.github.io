Cosmology

Matt Kowalski

9/24/08

Cosmology is the study of the universe as a whole.  The main questions cosmologists try to answer are: What happened at the beginning of the universe? How old is the universe? What is the size and geometry of the universe? And ultimately, how will the universe end?

A possible way the universe started is the Big Bang. This is what is believed to have happened today, where all of the matter and energy in the universe were in the same place. Unfortunately, we can't know what happened before or at the moment of the Big Bang with physics we currently have because at that moment, the universe would have had zero space, infinite density, and infinite temperature, and the laws of physics break down because of it.

As for the age of the universe, there are two possible ways to try and discover this. The first way is to find and date the oldest object in the universe. Knowing that, we know that the universe is at least that old. Currently, the oldest known objects are globular star clusters in the Milky Way dated to be around 13-14 billion years old. A second approach to dating the universe is using Hubble's Law. Hubble's Law is pretty simple, and says the velocity of an object traveling through space is equal to a constant H, known as Hubble's Constant, multiplied by a change of distance. To use this to date the universe we must realize that at some point our galaxy and another galaxy were at the same point. From that we can use this law to determine how long we have been apart based on our distance away from each other.

There are three types of universes. The first expands from the Big Bang, then comes back together in a Big Crunch, or Gnab Gib (Big Bang backwards). This universe is called a closed universe because it has positive spatial curvature, finite volume, and no boundaries. The second universe type expands from the Big Bang and continues to expand, but at time goes to infinity, the velocity of the universe's expansion goes to zero. This universe is called a flat universe because it is spatially flat, has infinite volume, and expands forever. Lastly, the final universe expands forever and if know an an open universe because it has infinite volume, negative spatial curvature, and infinite space.

So what kind of universe do we live in? We think we live in an open universe. There are ways to find out for sure but most of the ways are impractical. One more practical way is to relate the expansion of the universe now with the universe's expansion in the distant past by looking at galaxies far, far away. This would work because the light from the galaxies in the past is just coming to us now. Another way is to measure geometrical properties of the universe by making a very large triangle and seeing if the sum of the angles is 180 degrees. A third way, which is also probably the best way as well, is to measure the angular sizes of galaxies, and depending on what you see, it may show different spatial curvature.

Unfortunately there is a lot of uncertainty in all we know due to many factors. One big factor is the existence of dark matter and its properties. We know of its existence because objects seem to have more mass than the visible matter should allow and that mass comes from dark matter.

Another big question in cosmology today is how the structure of the universe came to be. For example, why are there galaxies, how come they form the way they do, and why are they where they are in space and not all together?
