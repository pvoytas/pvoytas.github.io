Space Physics/ Space Weather

  Rebecca Cooper

9/24/08

  • Space Weather refers to the adverse impact of upper-atmosphere disturbances on terrestrial technological systems.

  • Cause of space weather is solar activity

  • coronal mass ejections, huge bubble of gas expelled from sun out into space; several billion tons of gas headed straight for Earth

  • The total energy contained in these high speed charged gases during a large event is roughly the same as a large solar flare (approximately equivalent to the total energy of 100 hurricanes) but expelled over a longer time period than a solar flare (many hours as compared to tens of minutes).

  • The sun is flinging 1 million tons of matter out into space every second: solar wind

  • solar wind hitting against the Earth's protective magnetic shield (the magnetosphere) in space is responsible for spaceweather storms.

  • The Earth's magnetic field protects us from dangerous radiation in space. High energy electrons and ions are forced to circle around the Earth at high altitudes, trapped in two donut shaped regions called the Van Allen Belts or Radiation Belts.

  • High speed ions and electrons travel along magnetic field lines and impact the atmosphere in the region called the auroral oval. Atmospheric gases are excited by these high speed collisions and give off different types of light as they relax back to their normal states. The color and altitude of the aurora tell us which atmospheric gases are being excited.

RESEARCH:

  • The Effects on the Ionosphere:

    • from solar wind interaction with the ionosphere (a layer of ions and electrons ~60km above Earth's surface), electron densities fluctuate: the more violent the solar wind, the greater the electron densities, which effects the path of signals from GPS satellites (important for navigation systems for planes and ships etc.)

    • MIT Haystack Observatory uses both Incoherent Scatter Radars and GPS satellites to observe densities. Their concern is the TEC values (which is what you get when you integrate a 1 meter x 1 meter column from the surface of the Earth to whatever altitude of interest)

    • further understanding of the effect on ionospheric electron densities allows for corrections to GPS signals, allowing for more accurate navigation

  • Magnetic Field Structure of Planets

    • Magnetometers designed and fabricated at UCLA have enabled space science faculty and students to greatly expand our understanding of the magnetic structure and dynamics of the Earth, Venus, Jupiter, their satellites, asteroids, and the solar wind.

    • The magnetometer on Galileo discovered Ganymede's intrinsic magnetic field and provided compelling evidence of a liquid water ocean below Europa's icy crust.

    • Presently operating experiments include magnetometers on the International Solar Terrestrial Program (ISTP) Polar spacecraft launched in 1996, and the Fast Auroral Snapshot Explorer (FAST), launched in 1995.
