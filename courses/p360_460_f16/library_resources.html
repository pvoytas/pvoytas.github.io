Resources for Junior/Senior seminar talks

 

Note: In general, access to much content on many of these sites is restricted to subscribers. Since the Wittenberg library has subscriptions to many (and access agreements to many), if you log in from a campus computer OR use a secure Wittenberg connection (wireless, other) with your personal computer, you will have greatly expanded access to content.

 

General library physics resources

 

Suggestions for places to start:

Magazines/General Sites:

  • Scientific American
  • Physics Today (American Institute of Physics ), particularly Physics Update
  • Physics World (Institute of Physics)
  • BBC News: Science and Environment
  • NYT Science
  • PhysOrg.com

Teaching journals

American Journal of Physics (American Association of Physics Teachers)

The Physics Teacher (AAPT)

 

Peer-reviewed journals/scholarly summaries*

  • Physics--highlights of exceptional papers within the body of excellent research " (from the American Physical Society--a good place to get a less technical summary of peer reviewed work)
  • Nature (the library subscribes to the print journal, online access somewhat limited, complete holdings found under here )
  • Science (the library subscribes to the print journal, online access somewhat limited)
  • American Scientist (Sigma Xi)
  • Physical Review Journals (APS)
  • AIP Journals (American Institute of physics)
  • Electronic Journal Center (Library listing of electronic journals we have access to)

Finding scholarly articles

  • Articles on a specific topic, or articles that cite a given paper: Science Citation Index  . (has lots of capability for narrowing by author, date, type of journal etc.)
  • Google scholar (scholar.google.com). You probably want to un-check the "include patents" option.
  • http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abstract_service.html (astro-related)
  • Preprint archive (archiv.org) pre-publication documents (preprints), other, not necessarily peer reviewed: use with caution.

Many of the sources above have associated social media sites, email options for tables of content, or RSS feeds (see individual sites for more about these or ask me).
