Astrophysics

 

Jason Barkeloo – Physics Minute – October 15, 2008


Astrophysics is the branch of astronomy that deals with the physics of the universe and the physical properties of the universe such as luminosity, density, temperature, and chemical composition of celestial objects such as stars, galaxies, and the interstellar medium, as well as their interactions. In general this is a very broad discipline of physics that is capable of including many other disciplines.


Some of the interactions studied and current research in the area include how infant stars grow from the disc that surround them. The mechanisms involved in this growth are studied using different techniques. Interferometry, which uses light from two or more telescopes together to obtain a higher level of detail of the object being looked at, this makes the equivalent image as though it was looked at from a telescope with a lens with diameter of the separation of the two, or more, telescopes.


Another recent area of research many aspects involve direct observation of certain galaxies, using Hubble observations of local galaxies we are able to tell more about how these all of these galaxies were formed by knowing certain facts about a few stars, such as their brightness and color, subtle features about a galaxy’s shape can be found.
