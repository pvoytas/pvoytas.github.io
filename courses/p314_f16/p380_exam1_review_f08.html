Physics 380 exam 1 Review, fall 2008

Written exam: Tuesday, November 18

For the written part, questions will emphasize the material covered in the HW assignments and reading. Material that you worked on in lab or homework should be understood at a deeper level (“how does this work?”) than material you just read about (“what is this?”).

Fundamental concepts include:

  • binary numbers/arithmetic
  • bits, bytes, nibbles
  • two’s complement, binary coded decimal
  • Logic Families (e.g. TTL, CMOS, ECL)
  • 3-state logic

Combinational logic:

  • gates
  • truth tables (what they are, how to use them)
  • Boolean algebra
  • building things from NAND gates

Sequential logic:

  • flip-flops (RS, JK, D)
  • counters (ripple, synchronous)
  • shift registers
  • one-shots, multiplexers

You will be provided with truth tables for various gates & flip-flops and the table of Boolean theorems.
