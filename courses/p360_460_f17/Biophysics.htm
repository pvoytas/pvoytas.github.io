Biophysics

Biophysics is that branch of knowledge that applies the principles of physics and chemistry and the methods of mathematical analysis and computer modeling to understand how biological systems work. Put simply, it’s the study of biological phenomena using the principles and techniques of physics. It basically focuses on the physics of organisms and their biological structures and processes. Biophysics is a molecular science which seeks to explain biological functions in terms of the molecular structures and properties of specific molecules. These molecules are the building blocks of living organisms which assemble into cells, tissues, and organisms.

There are several different subfields in biophysics such as physiological, theoretical, and functional biomechanics. Physiological biophysics explains various biological processes such as the transmission of nerve impulses and the mechanisms of muscle contraction and sight. Theoretical uses mathematical and physical models to explain life processes. Functional biomechanics helps us understand the human body as a whole by looking at the relationships and interactions between the various body parts and systems that contribute to its ability to function.

A lot of the scientific success of biophysics depends on its ability to develop detailed physical mechanisms to explain specific biological processes. For example, the double helical structure of DNA provides a framework for an explanation of how genetic material is replicated and of how genetic mutations arise. Specific proteins mediate the unwinding of DNA and the assembly of a new strand based on complemetary base pairing of guanine with cytosine and adenine with thymine. If there is one mismatch between these pairs, a mutation of the gene occurs.

In functional biomechanics, there are two types of functional activity that the human body performs, global and core. There are many core functional activities that are essential to performing global functional activities. For example, running, jumping, reaching, pushing, walking, and throwing along with many other activities all combine together in order to allow global activities to occur such as doing things around the house, getting ready in the morning, and recreational and sport activities. Something as simple as standing and balancing is actually quite complex when it’s broken down into the different aspects that are required for this action to take place. Biomechanics evaluates the motion of living organisms and the actions and forces on it. It’s a combination of several different fields including anatomy, kinematics, kinesiology and kinetics.
