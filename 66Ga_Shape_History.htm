66Ga Shape History

Words:

There are 4 existing (3 decent statistics) measurements of the gallium spectrum shape. These are:

Phys.Rev. 129, 1782 (1963)

D.C.Camp, L.M.Langer

Shape of the 0+ ? 0+ Positron Spectrum in Ga66


Phys.Rev. 148, 1024 (1966)

G.S.Sidhu, J.B.Gerhart

Observation of the 4.12-MeV O14 A N14 Positron Spectrum Shape


Nucl.Phys. A98, 585 (1967)

L.van Neste, R.Coussement, J.P.Deutsch

The (0+)-(0+) Positon Decay of 66Ga


Nucl.Instrum.Methods 133, 457 (1976)

R.B.Moore, S.I.Hayakawa, D.M.Rehfield

A High Energy Beta Spectrometer Using an Intrinsic Germanium Crystal in a Super-Conducting Solenoid

These were all thesis projects, and we have copies of the theses (VanNeste's in Dutch :( )

Camp and Langer measured the shape and found a deviation from the allowed shape (including an extra, completely unphysically motivated (1+b/w) term). Only shape factor graphs presented. We obtained the shape factor data by digitizing his graph.

Sidhu measured gallium to check their spectrometer. Kurie plot and shape factor graphs both presented. They found no significant deviation from the allowed shape. We digitized both the Kurie plot and the shape factor plot.

van Neste measured results which claimed to refute the un-physical term camp and langer claimed, but did observe non-allowed shape as well. No spectra or Kurie plots, only shape factor graphs and tables of coefficients. Unfortunately, the shape factors that seem to be indicated in the figures in this work cannot be obtained from the coefficients of the shape factor reported in tables in the work. We obtained the shape factor coefficients by digitizing his graphs and fitting with a function of the form 1+a*w+b*w^2+c/w.

Rehfield claims to have measured a spectrum in complete agreement with the van Neste shape factor. Only Kurie plot presented. In this case, there were hundreds of data points, and an excellent (by eye) linear fit to the data. We therefore only digitized the line and worked from there.

What we have done is tried to reconstruct back to a form we can compare to an allowed spectrum. In general our procedure has been as follows:

when we have only a shape factor (=N(w,Z)/(F(z,w)*p^2*(w-w0)^2) ), we have multiplied back out to get N(w,Z), using a modern fermi function (since it's not always clear what people used, and we find small—(not fully quantified)--differences between our modern function and typical functions used at those times).
