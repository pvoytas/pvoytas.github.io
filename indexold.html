Research and Scholarly Interests

  • I have primarily been studying the Weak Interaction. This is the fundamental interaction responsible for (among other things) nuclear beta decay and is often just referred to as "the fourth fundamental force of nature" (to go along with gravity, electromagnetism, and the strong nuclear force). Much of my actual experimental work has involved using lasers to manipulate radioactive atoms in order to study their properties or beta decays in more detail, with more precision and control. This has involved work at the Experimental Nuclear Physics Group and the University of Wisconsin-Madison as well as work at the SUNY Stony Brook Nuclear Physics Lab.
  • Recently, my work has involved the electron capture decay of 7Be. This work by me and Chris Ternovan ('00) involves a collaboration with the TWINSOL project (located at the University of Notre Dame Nuclear Structure Lab) and the X-ray Astrophysics group at the University of Wisconsin Madison. A paper on the results in PDF format is available here.
  • I am involved in the Observatory for Multiflavor NeutrInos from Supernovae (OMNIS) which will be a neutrino detector for neutrinos from supernovas in our galaxy.
  • I am also involved, with Dr. Elizabeth George (Wittenberg) and collaborators at Uinversity of Wisconsin-Madison and University of Washington on a project to study the weak interaction in the beta decay of 14O.
