Research and Scholarly Interests

  • I am involved, with Dr. Elizabeth George (Wittenberg) and collaborators at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, on a project to study the weak interaction in the beta decay of 14O.
  • I am also involved, with Dr. George on the phenomenon of Light Induced Atom Desorption.
  • I have primarily been studying the Weak Interaction. This is the fundamental interaction responsible for (among other things) nuclear beta decay and is often just referred to as "the fourth fundamental force of nature" (to go along with gravity, electromagnetism, and the strong nuclear force). Much of my actual experimental work has involved using lasers to manipulate radioactive atoms in order to study their properties or beta decays in more detail, with more precision and control. This has involved work at the University of Wisconsin-Madison as well as work at the SUNY Stony Brook Nuclear Physics Lab. Commentary by Luis Orozco on Francium.
  • My earlier work has involved the electron capture decay of 7Be. This work by me and Chris Ternovan ('00) involved a collaboration with the TWINSOLproject (located at the University of Notre Dame Institute for Structure and Nuclear AstrophysicsNuclear Structure Lab) and the X-ray Astrophysics group at the University of Wisconsin Madison. A paper on the results in PDF format is available here.
